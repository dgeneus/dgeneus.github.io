Plywood

Plywood is typically produced from fast-growing, sustainable wood species, and its efficient use of wood resources makes it an environmentally favorable choice for many applications. The manufacturing process involves slicing logs into thin veneers, which are then bonded under heat and pressure to form panels of precise dimensions.
Source: Plywood as A Construction Material

Hardwood

We use hardwood (approx. 1,700 lbf, 7,560 N hardness), which is naturally decay-resistant and more durable than other construction woods. Our hardwood is sourced from sustainable cultivators in the Appalachian region and the Midwest.
Source: The Rise of Black Locust Lumber

The Science

Wood is a natural composite material primarily composed of cellulose fibers (which provide strength) embedded in a matrix of lignin (which binds the fibers and provides rigidity). This unique structure gives wood a remarkable combination of strength, flexibility, and lightweight properties, making it ideal for construction.
Source: Wood Handbook, Wood as an Engineering Material
